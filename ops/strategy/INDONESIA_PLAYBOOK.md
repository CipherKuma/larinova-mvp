## 2. Product

Larinova is an all-in-one practice management and AI-powered automation platform built specifically for Indonesian healthcare professionals. The product is available fully in Bahasa Indonesia and English.

### Core Modules:

**Practice Management**
- Patient records and history management
- Appointment scheduling and consultation tracking
- Task management for doctors and clinic staff
- Full operational management layer (comparable to a healthcare-specific ERP)

**AI Scribe**
- Real-time transcription and summarization of doctor-patient consultations
- Automatic SOAP note and medical document generation from spoken consultations
- Supports Indonesian language natively — this is the primary technical differentiator; no direct competitor in Indonesia offers this

**Document Automation**
- Auto-generates prescriptions, referral letters, medical certificates, and other standard clinical documents post-consultation
- Eliminates manual documentation time

**Workflow Automation**
- Zapier-like automation engine connecting modules (e.g., consultation complete → patient record updated → documents generated → follow-up reminder triggered)
- Reduces administrative overhead for solo practitioners and clinic operations teams

### Key Differentiators:
- Only full-Indonesian-language clinical management platform in the market
- All-in-one (replaces fragmented tools: separate scheduling apps, note-taking apps, document generators)
- AI-native — automation is core, not a feature add-on
- Designed around Indonesian healthcare workflows, not adapted from Western products

## 6. Lead Sources & Prioritization

**Priority 1 — Warm network (highest conversion probability)**
Gabriel has personal contacts in the Indonesian medical space. Strategy: convert a known doctor to an active user → use them as a reference and referral source → expand through their professional network. Doctor-to-doctor word of mouth in Indonesia is extremely high trust.

**Priority 2 — WhatsApp outreach**
Primary cold and warm outreach channel. Indonesia has near-universal WhatsApp penetration among professionals. All outreach should default to WhatsApp. Messages must be short, warm, and conversational — Indonesian doctors respond poorly to formal or corporate-sounding messages.

**Priority 3 — In-person hospital/clinic visits**
For larger institutions (clinic chains, hospitals), direct visits to speak with decision-makers (hospital directors, medical directors, operations managers) are effective. Bring a phone to demo the product and leave a one-page summary with the sign-up link.

**Priority 4 — LinkedIn**
Use for reaching institutional decision-makers (hospital administrators, clinic owners, directors) — not individual doctors. Search in Indonesian: "direktur rumah sakit," "manajer klinik." Connect → brief message → WhatsApp handoff if possible.

**Priority 5 — Email**
Use as follow-up channel if WhatsApp yields no response. Not a primary channel.

**Cold calling:** Lower priority given the Indonesian market preference for WhatsApp, but viable for institutional targets where a direct line is available.

## 7. Outreach Sequence & Process

**Standard sequence for every new prospect:**

1. WhatsApp introduction message
2. If interested → send demo video link
3. After video → send sign-up form link
4. No response after 2 days → one follow-up message
5. Still no response → move on (maximum 2 touchpoints per prospect)
6. Post sign-up → activation follow-up to ensure they use the app
7. Post-activation → feedback request + referral ask

**Key outreach principles:**
- Messages must be short — doctors are time-constrained; long messages are ignored
- Tone: warm and human, not corporate or salesy
- Language: Bahasa Indonesia by default
- Never hard sell or create pressure
- Referral ask should always follow a positive interaction
