Section 2

Problem & market

The clinical workflow problem

An Indian outpatient doctor sees 30+ patients per day. After every consult, they must produce a SOAP note, an ICD-10 code, and a prescription — typed manually into an EMR or written by hand. Most EMRs are designed in English. When a Tamil-speaking patient describes symptoms in Tamil, the doctor performs mental translation while typing, and clinical nuance is lost in transit. The cumulative documentation overhead averages 90 minutes per doctor per day — either at the cost of patient time, or of going home on time. Existing AI scribes partially solve documentation in English and Hindi, but completely fail to serve the Tamil-, Telugu-, Bengali-, and Marathi-speaking practices that make up the majority of clinical India.

The market

India has approximately 1.1 million doctors. Eka.Care, the only AI scribe operating in India, has reached approximately 65,000 doctors as of 2025 — almost entirely English- and Hindi-speaking. That leaves roughly a million doctors unserved by AI scribing, including the entire Tamil, Telugu, Bengali, and Marathi-speaking population. Larinova's Day-1 wedge is the ~140,000 Tamil-speaking doctors in Tamil Nadu, Puducherry and parts of Karnataka — a market with zero direct AI scribe alternative.

Section 3

Solution & product

Larinova is one platform that handles the entire outpatient journey — from the patient booking the slot to the wellness follow-up seven days after the consult — built natively in Tamil first.

The five layers

  1. Booking. Patients book a consultation slot from a public page on the doctor's domain. They self-confirm by email; the doctor's calendar updates instantly.
  2. AI-guided intake. Before the appointment, the patient fills a structured form. An AI agent follows up for missing details and accepts lab uploads, so the doctor walks in with complete information.
  3. 60-second Prep Brief. The moment the patient arrives, the doctor sees a one-screen brief: presenting symptoms, prior medications, flagged red flags, and a structured chronology drawn from prior visits and intake responses.
  4. Tamil-native consultation scribe. The doctor speaks naturally in Tamil, Hindi, English, or any mix. Powered by Sarvam AI's Indic clinical models, Larinova generates a SOAP note, an ICD-10 code, and a draft prescription in real time. The doctor reviews, edits, and signs in under two minutes.
  5. Wellness follow-up agent. A conversational agent checks in with the patient on day 1, day 3, and day 7. Replies are classified — only flagged ones reach the doctor. Routine recovery never bothers the doctor.

Technology infrastructure

Larinova is built on Next.js with Supabase as the data layer, deployed on Vercel. Real-time Tamil and Hindi transcription runs on Sarvam AI's WebSocket streaming API. Clinical reasoning and SOAP-note generation route through a dedicated Claude Service infrastructure (claude.fierypools.fun) which centralises and caps unit cost across our AI inference. Asynchronous workflows (intake, dispatch, follow-up) are orchestrated through Inngest, a durable event-driven queue. The product is a Progressive Web App — works on every doctor's phone or tablet without an install — with offline fallback wired via Serwist. ABDM and ABHA integration are native; the company is DPDP Act 2023 compliant by design.

Why this stack matters to an investor: capital-efficient, modern, vendor-neutral. We do not pay platform rent to a competitor (no Microsoft Health Bot, no Google Cloud Healthcare API). Our AI inference is layered behind our own service, so we can swap models without product changes. This means our gross margin compounds as model costs fall.

Competition & moat

Larinova has one indirect competitor in India (Eka.Care), a fragmented adjacent market in Indonesia (clinic-management tools without AI scribe), and a set of premium global scribes (Nuance, Suki, Abridge) who do not operate in Indian or Indonesian markets and are priced for US healthcare systems. None of them deliver what Larinova delivers, in any market, at any price.

Wellness follow-up agent Day 1, 3, 7 conversational No No
ABDM / ABHA integration Full native Partial Not applicable
Price per doctor / month ₹999 ~₹1,100 ₹17,000 – ₹70,000

Why the moat is structural

Eka.Care has chosen to ship English and Hindi for three years. Adding Tamil clinical AI is not a feature flip — it requires integration with Sarvam's Indic clinical models, retraining on Tamil clinical corpora, and clinical-domain validation. We expect at least 12–18 months of head-start. By the time Eka.Care could ship a Tamil scribe, we will have the Tamil-doctor relationships, the ambassador network, the hospital pilots, and the regulatory integrations locked in — and we will have moved on to Telugu, Bengali, and Marathi.

Global scribes face a different barrier: they price for US healthcare economics and do not have the local presence, regulatory integration (ABDM/ABHA), or Indic language coverage to compete in India. Their entry would require a complete repricing and re-architecture — not a rational priority for them given their US growth runway.

The most credible competitive risk is not from a competitor but from doctor adoption speed. Indian doctors are habit-bound; even the right tool takes time to displace manual notes. Our mitigations are co-building with our 5 alpha doctors weekly to drive the product to obvious-better, the free trial to remove cost friction, and the ambassador program to create peer pull.
