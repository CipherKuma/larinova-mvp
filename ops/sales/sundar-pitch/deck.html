The problem

Indian doctors are losing 90 minutes a day to a typewriter — and the patient's language to translation.

Every Indian GP sees 30+ patients a day. Every consultation generates a SOAP note, an ICD-10 code, and a prescription that has to be typed into an EMR designed in English. Tamil-speaking patients describe symptoms in Tamil. The doctor mentally translates as they type. Clinical nuance is lost in that translation.

90 min/day
Typed manually after 30+ consultations — a full hour and a half stolen from patients or from going home
22 languages
Spoken by Indian patients in OPDs every day. Existing AI EMRs support only English and Hindi.
~65K doctors
On Eka.Care today — the only AI scribe in India. English / Hindi only. Scribe-only, no booking, no follow-up.
5 disconnected apps
Most clinics juggle separate tools for booking, intake, EMR, scribe, and follow-up — none of them talk to each other
The Tamil-language clinical fidelity gap is not a feature — it is a clinical accuracy problem. Mental translation under time pressure produces worse notes, worse prescriptions, and worse outcomes. No platform in the Indian market today closes that gap.

The solution

One platform for the entire OPD — booking to follow-up.

Larinova is the only end-to-end OPD platform for Indian doctors. Patients book online, complete an AI-guided intake before they arrive, and the doctor walks in with a 60-second Prep Brief in hand. During the consult, Larinova transcribes Tamil / Hindi / English speech and generates SOAP notes, ICD-10 codes, and a signed prescription in real time. After the visit, an agent follows up at days 1, 3, and 7.

The product

A doctor's full clinic day, in 9 minutes of total admin.

Larinova replaces the 90 minutes of post-consult documentation with a 90-second review-and-sign per patient. The doctor speaks naturally — Tamil, Hindi, English, or mixed. Larinova handles the rest. Live in production at app.larinova.com — Sundar can sign up and try it himself.

A typical Tamil consult — what the doctor experiences
9:58 AM
Patient arrives. Prep Brief is on doctor's screen — symptoms, prior meds, two flagged red flags.
10:00 AM
Consult begins. Doctor presses Start Recording. Larinova listens in Tamil.
10:11 AM
Consult ends. SOAP note, ICD-10 code, and prescription appear on screen — in English, drafted from the Tamil conversation.
10:13 AM
Doctor reviews, edits one line, signs. Patient leaves with a signed Rx via email. Total admin: under 2 min.
+1 day
Wellness agent checks in with the patient: "How are you feeling today?" Replies are classified — only flagged ones reach the doctor.
Every line above is live in production today. Larinova is a Progressive Web App — works on every doctor's phone or tablet, no install required. Service worker verified, offline fallback wired.

Indonesia · The second engine

India is primary. Indonesia is validated optionality.

India is Larinova's first market and remains the focus of this round. But the same Larinova landing page is live in Bahasa Indonesia at larinova.com/id, and the opportunity in Indonesia is structurally even cleaner: 180,000+ doctors and zero direct AI scribe competitor. We treat it as the second engine — designed to validate a cross-border thesis without diluting India focus.

Why Indonesia is the cleanest second market in healthcare AI

Indonesian clinic management software (Klinik Pintar, Assist.id, Trustmedis) costs Rp 250K–3M/month and does scheduling and records — but none auto-generate clinical notes. There is no Bahasa-native AI scribe in the Indonesian market today. The closest entrant — Medow Health AI from Singapore — has not launched in Indonesia. Global premium scribes (Nuance, Suki) cost $200–$830/month per doctor and have no Bahasa support. Larinova at IDR 299K/month (~$18) is structurally uncatchable on price + language.
