Medical Certificate Standards and Larinova Samples

Date: 2026-05-06

Purpose: Show the Indian-format certificate fields now supported by Larinova and provide samples generated from the current Larinova certificate builder.

1. Indian Format Checklist

The Indian Medical Council professional conduct regulations recommend that medical certificates record:

  • Patient signature or thumb impression.
  • Patient address.
  • At least one patient identification mark.
  • Certificate prepared in the recommended leave/fitness format.
  • Doctor registration number on certificates.
  • Doctor signature and seal before external use.
  • Copy/details retained in a medical-certificate register.
  • Truthful, non-misleading clinical content.

2. Larinova Certificate Controls

Larinova's certificate flow is designed around doctor control:

  • Certificate is created only inside an authenticated doctor workflow.
  • Patient is selected from the doctor-linked patient record.
  • Patient age, sex, and address are pulled from the patient record.
  • Doctor name, degrees, registration number, council, clinic, and phone are included.
  • Certificate includes examination date, place, patient signature/thumb, and identification marks.
  • Certificate remains a draft until the doctor verifies and signs/stamps it.
  • Certificate content is saved to the document record, creating an internal copy/register trail.

3. Sample Certificates Generated From Larinova

The following sample outputs use demo patient and doctor details. They are not real medical certificates and must not be used externally without physician review, patient verification, doctor signature, and clinic/hospital seal.
