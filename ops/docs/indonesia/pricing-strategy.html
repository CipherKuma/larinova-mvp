Market Overview
Indonesia healthcare software landscape

The opportunity

Indonesia's healthcare digitisation is at an inflection point. The Ministry of Health's SATUSEHAT mandate requires all healthcare facilities to connect to the national health data platform. As of early 2025, 98.6% of institutions have registered — but most are running outdated, clunky software with zero AI capability. The compliance window is the wedge. The doctor productivity gap is the moat.

Why Larinova Wins
Differentiation and value proposition

The three unfair advantages

1. AI medical scribe — a completely uncontested lane

Not a single competitor in Indonesia has AI transcription, voice-to-notes, or auto form-fill. This is not a feature gap — it is a category gap. Every doctor using a competitor today still manually types every patient note. A busy doctor seeing 30 patients/day spends roughly 90–120 minutes on documentation. Larinova eliminates most of that. The ROI is not marginal — it is transformative.

2. Per-doctor pricing unlocks a new buyer

Every competitor sells to the clinic owner or hospital administrator. The purchase decision sits in procurement, goes through committee, and takes months. Larinova's per-doctor model lets us sell directly to the doctor — who experiences the pain personally and has authority to expense a tool that saves them 2 hours a day. This collapses the sales cycle from months to days and enables bottom-up viral growth within a clinic.

3. Communications infrastructure nobody else has built

No competitor offers email campaigns, patient CRM, multi-channel reminders, or a doctor collaboration feed. Indonesian patients are WhatsApp-native. Indonesian doctors are undersupported on peer consultation. These are solved problems in Western healthcare software (Epic, Athenahealth, Elation) that have never been brought to this market. Larinova brings the full stack.

Pricing Architecture
Tiers, model logic, and rationale

Pricing model: platform base fee + per-doctor seat

The hybrid model (base + per-seat) is used by best-in-class healthcare SaaS globally (Athenahealth, Kareo, DrChrono). The base fee anchors the platform's intrinsic value independent of headcount. The per-doctor fee scales revenue naturally as clinics grow. This model avoids the race-to-the-bottom of pure per-facility pricing while remaining accessible to solo practitioners through a zero-base-fee Core tier.

Government & Public Sector
Special pricing strategy for Puskesmas & government hospitals

Why the government segment matters

Indonesia has approximately 10,000+ Puskesmas (community health centres) and 900+ government hospitals (RSUD/RSUP). These facilities serve the majority of Indonesia's 270 million patients. They also face the strongest SATUSEHAT compliance pressure — Kemenkes mandates integration and these facilities cannot afford to fail audits. This creates a real entry point despite near-zero software budgets.

The strategic play is not to compete on price with SIKDA Optima (Rp 300k/month for a barebones tool). It is to offer a meaningfully better product at a price that can be covered by government health IT budget allocations (DIPA/BOK) or through a grant-subsidy partnership model.

Go-to-market strategy for government segment

Phase 1 — Prove with private clinics first (now – Q3 2025)

Do not lead with the government segment. Government procurement in Indonesia is slow, political, and margin-destroying if entered too early. Build the product, prove SATUSEHAT compliance, and collect 10–20 private clinic case studies with measurable outcomes.

Phase 2 — Partner with a SIMRS vendor or distributor (Q4 2025)

Rather than direct sales into government, partner with an existing SIMRS vendor that already has government relationships (e.g., a Dinkes partner). White-label the AI scribe module as a bolt-on they can resell. This gets you into government without the procurement burden.

Phase 3 — Register on e-katalog / LKPP (2026)

Indonesia's government procurement platform (e-katalog) allows pre-approved vendors to sell directly to government buyers without full tender. Register Larinova as an approved health IT vendor. This is the path to scale in the government segment.

Phase 4 — Pilot with Kemenkes or Dinkes (2026–2027)

Target a Dinas Kesehatan (regional health office) for a subsidised pilot. A successful Dinkes endorsement creates top-down pressure on all Puskesmas in that region to adopt the platform. This is how SIKDA Optima grew in Jakarta — Dinkes DKI mandate.

Critical warning on government pricing

Government contracts in Indonesia are won on price and relationships, not features. The moment you enter the government segment with aggressive discounts, you risk anchoring your brand as a budget product — which undermines private clinic pricing. Keep government pricing clearly separate from private tier pricing, use a different product SKU (e.g., 'Larinova Public'), and never publicly advertise the government rate. The two markets should not know what the other is paying.

What 400 active doctors looks like

400 doctors across Indonesia is a very achievable Year 1 target. Indonesia has 180,000+ registered doctors. Capturing 0.22% of that base in 12 months requires winning roughly 40–60 clinics. At an average of 7 doctors per clinic, that is 6–8 new clinics per month from Month 6 onward. That is a sales motion one founder-led BD person can execute.

Key pricing levers to pull as you scale

Annual pre-payment incentive

Offer 2 months free on annual commits. At Rp 650k × 12 = Rp 7.8M per doctor, a 2-month free incentive costs you Rp 1.3M but locks in Rp 6.5M guaranteed revenue. Dramatically improves cash flow predictability.

Referral programme

Doctors talk to each other. A Rp 200k/month credit per referred doctor who activates is a powerful growth loop. At a 20% referral conversion rate, CAC drops to near zero within high-density hospital networks.

Usage-based upsell

Core tier doctors who hit 50 AI scribe sessions in week 3 will upgrade themselves. Build the upgrade nudge into the product — not a sales call. This is the most important conversion mechanism in the pricing architecture.

Clinic-level volume pricing

For clinics with 10+ doctors on Pro, offer a 10% volume discount. It prevents churn at the enterprise threshold and keeps them in the Pro tier rather than shopping Enterprise.
