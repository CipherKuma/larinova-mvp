Market Overview
India healthcare software landscape

The scale of the opportunity

India is the world's largest democracy with 1.4 billion people and a healthcare sector projected to reach $650 billion by 2025. The country has 1.1 million registered doctors, over 70,000 hospitals, and roughly 600,000 clinics. Digital adoption in healthcare is at an inflection point — driven by the government's Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission (ABDM), rising smartphone penetration, and a post-COVID push toward teleconsultation and EMR. Yet the vast majority of private clinics still run on paper or rudimentary software. The gap between what exists and what's needed is enormous.

The Eka.Care Problem
The one AI competitor you must know deeply

Why Eka.Care is different from every other competitor

Every other Indian EMR vendor is irrelevant to the AI scribe discussion. Eka.Care is not. Founded in 2021 by the ex-CTO of Goibibo, Eka.Care has built EkaScribe — an AI-powered voice assistant that transcribes doctor-patient conversations into structured clinical notes. They have 65,000 active doctors. They have ABDM compliance. They have a 12-language patient app. They are the only direct AI competitor in India. This section is entirely about how to beat them.

How to beat Eka.Care

Language depth is the kill shot

Eka.Care's patient app supports 12 languages but their clinical scribe is primarily English and Hindi. India has 22 official languages and hundreds of dialects. A Tamil-speaking cardiologist in Chennai, a Bengali-speaking GP in Kolkata, a Telugu surgeon in Hyderabad — none of them are well-served by Eka.Care's scribe. Sarvam AI's Indic language models cover all 22 official languages with clinical-grade accuracy. This is Larinova's moat that cannot be replicated quickly.

Full HMS vs documentation tool

Eka.Care is fundamentally a documentation tool that added some EMR features. Their inpatient, pharmacy, lab, and accounting modules are weak. A clinic needs more than a scribe — they need billing, pharmacy, CRM, and automation. Larinova is the full stack. Eka.Care is a feature within a full stack. This makes us the platform they eventually need to replace Eka.Care with.

Price and feature combination

At ₹899/doctor/month for Core vs Eka.Care's estimated ₹999–₹2,999, Larinova enters lower and delivers more. The comparison should be: 'Same AI scribe quality, all 22 languages, plus full clinic management, patient CRM, and email campaigns — for less.' That is a straightforward win in a price-sensitive market.

Why Larinova Wins
Differentiation and the language moat

The four unfair advantages in India

1. The language moat — 22 Indian languages, fully supported

India's linguistic diversity is a feature, not a bug — if you embrace it. No competitor offers full clinical support (not just UI, but actual scribe transcription and note generation) in Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Malayalam, Bengali, Marathi, Gujarati, Punjabi, Odia, Assamese, and the other 12 official languages. Powered by Sarvam AI's Indic language models, Larinova is the only platform where a Malayalam-speaking doctor in Kerala can dictate in Malayalam and get structured FHIR-compliant clinical notes in Malayalam or English. This is not a feature that can be shipped in a quarter — it requires deep language model work that gives Larinova a multi-year head start.

2. AI scribe with auto form-fill — Eka.Care can transcribe, we can fill

EkaScribe transcribes conversations. Larinova goes further: our AI listens, transcribes, extracts structured clinical data, and auto-fills the EMR form — diagnosis codes, vitals, prescriptions, follow-up dates. The doctor reviews and approves in 30 seconds instead of manually typing for 4 minutes. This is the difference between a transcription tool and a productivity platform. Every competitor comparison should anchor on this distinction.

3. Full HMS — not just a scribe

HealthPlix is EMR-only. Eka.Care is documentation-first. Practo Ray is appointments-first. None of them are the complete operating system for a clinic. Larinova covers the full workflow: registration, queue, EMR, billing, pharmacy, lab orders, CRM, email, automation, and analytics — all in one platform. Doctors who start on Core for the scribe will upgrade to Pro for the automation and CRM. This is the growth flywheel.

4. Doctor-to-doctor social layer — a category that doesn't exist

India has 1.1 million doctors who consult in near-isolation. There is no professional network built into a clinical tool. Larinova's doctor collaboration feed — for case sharing, specialist referrals, peer consultation, and CME — is a feature that Practo's marketplace has tried to serve externally but nobody has embedded inside an EMR. This creates retention and network effects that no competitor can replicate without rebuilding their entire product.

Government & Public Sector
Special pricing for PHCs, CHCs & government hospitals

The government segment in India — size and complexity

India's public healthcare system is massive: ~25,000 Primary Health Centres (PHCs), ~5,000 Community Health Centres (CHCs), ~750 District Hospitals, and hundreds of government medical college hospitals. The government also runs eSushrut Lite (NHA's own HMIS at ₹299/month) targeting small facilities — creating a difficult price floor. However, government facilities have real pain: compliance burden, poor tools, and the political pressure to meet ABDM targets. The entry angle is not price — it is ABDM compliance delivery and multilingual support for the rural healthcare workers who need it most.

Critical: eSushrut Lite is the floor, not the target

The NHA's own eSushrut Lite at ₹299/month means the Government of India has effectively set a free/near-free floor for PHCs. Larinova should not try to compete at this price point — it will destroy margins and brand positioning. Instead, position Larinova Public Starter at ₹199–₹399 as the premium government product with AI scribe and 22-language support that eSushrut Lite does not offer. The pitch: 'Same price, 10x the capability.'

4-phase government go-to-market strategy

Phase 1 — Private first, always (now – Q3 2025)

Never lead with government. Private clinics in Chennai, Bengaluru, Mumbai, and Delhi are the beachhead. Build 20–30 case studies with measurable outcomes before approaching any government body. Government procurement in India requires vendor track record, ABDM certification, and political relationships — none of which can be shortcut.

Phase 2 — NHA / ABDM certification (Q3–Q4 2025)

Get Larinova certified as an ABDM-compliant HIP/HIU. This is the single most important government credential. The certification process involves sandbox testing, a WASA security audit by a CERT-IN empanelled agency, and a demonstration in front of NHA. Once certified, Larinova can be promoted through all ABDM channels.

Phase 3 — State NHA partnerships (2026)

Each state has a State NHA / NHM office that manages healthcare IT budgets. Partner with 2–3 state NHAs for a subsidised pilot in their PHC network. Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, and Maharashtra are the highest-budget states with strong digital health ambitions. A successful state pilot creates a template for national scale.

Phase 4 — GeM / GFR procurement registration (2026)

Register Larinova on the Government e-Marketplace (GeM) portal under the Health IT category. GeM allows government buyers to procure directly without full tender. This is how to sell to District Hospitals and government clinics at scale without navigating L1-tender processes that are won on price alone.

Key pricing levers to pull as you scale

The freemium conversion trap

HealthPlix's free tier creates doctors who are used to paying nothing. Do NOT offer a permanent free tier — it devalues the product permanently. A 14-day full-featured trial is the correct model. Once a doctor has spent 14 days with an AI scribe that saves them 90 minutes a day, asking for ₹899/month is a formality.

Annual pre-payment incentive

₹899 × 12 = ₹10,788 per doctor per year. Offer 2 months free on annual commit (effective ₹764/month). This locks in ₹9,168 guaranteed and improves cash flow dramatically — critical for an early-stage company building runway.

Hospital network pricing

When a hospital network (like Narayana, Apollo, Fortis) wants Larinova across multiple facilities, negotiate a fixed per-doctor rate with a volume discount: 50–99 doctors = 15% off, 100–199 doctors = 25% off, 200+ = custom. This is how to land enterprise accounts while maintaining per-doctor pricing discipline.

Language-specific partnerships

Partner with state medical associations (Tamil Nadu Medical Association, Karnataka Medical Association) for group pricing. A state-level deal at ₹750/doctor/month for 500 doctors = ₹37.5L/month. These deals are won through relationship, not sales — make this a Year 2 play after the product is proven.
